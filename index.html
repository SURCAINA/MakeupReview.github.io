The Good, the bad, and the ugly...

There's a lot of products out there and it can get overwhelming picking from so many options of color stories, price points, brand, packaging, and etc. This will review almost all makeup that I've tried and you might find a review of that product you were thinking of getting. I'll make sure to link their pages so if you enjoy a product you can travel straight to it's page to look at other peoples feedback and more information on the product from the store!

Pressed Pigment Eyeshadow

Fade into Hue Eyeshadow Palette by ColourPop

Review:

This was the first palette I purchased for my collection and the pigment is beautiful. The Mattes and metallics aren't patchy and with the range in hues you can make so many eyeshadow looks. The only real cons of this palette are the glitters. The glitters are so loose that it doesn't really stick to the lid and you can't get it to stick to your brushes. As with all eyeshadow the pigment falls when you apply it so beware the power of pigment because it can and will stain. The packaging is pretty but it's a paper cover so pigment will stain it. 100% Would repurchase this item.

35$

Tip!

If you'd like to keep your palettes cleaner keep the clear plastic shadow protector that comes with it! It's easy to wipe off and protects the packaging from pigment stains!

Stone Cold Fox Eyeshadow Palette by ColourPop

Review:

Nice cool browns but some colors are a little less pigmented and require building. The black takes time to build and the white is very transparent. This palette has some pretty looking metallics however they don't apply smoothly which can be a problem if you're looking for a high glam pigmented look and want a really packed sparkle. Packaging has that same paper covering so be careful of pigment staining especially with the black eyeshadow. Wouldn't repurchase this due to the metallics opt for something more compact if you're looking for a neutral palette with some sparkle.

35$

Matte About Hue Eyeshadow Palette by ColourPop

Review:

One of my absoulute favorites ColourPop is amazing at creating matte eyeshadows. The color story is darker towards the bottom but they still include so many pretty pastels so you can blend your looks seamlessly. They're pigmented but need a little building to get the real vibrancy you see in the palette. Same paper covered packaging so be careful of staining. Would 100% repurchase this item.

35$

Trouble Maker Eyeshadow Palette by ColourPop

Review:

Given to me as a gift, the color story is a little scattered but the glitters are very beautiful Villain Era is my favorite and I use it all the time. A few of the shades are a color shift and you can utilize it to create a wonderful glam shifting look that looks different depending on your lighting. The pans are large and if you're looking for some shadow with a little more of a rebel feel this might be what you're looking for. The packaging is paper so watch out for pigment stains. Worth a try if you're in the market for some of these colors however I myself would not repurchase it because of how little I get to use their colors.

18$

High Tide Eyeshadow Palette by Colourpop -discontiued

Review:

Beautiful dark teals mixed with light turquoise to create some beautiful ocean looks. I love the combo of Flora and Just a Splash. Great for that oceanside pool feel in the form of makeup. Needs a little build and depending on the darkness of it comes off a little patchy at first. Paper packaging watch for staining. I wouldn't repurchase however since I don't use the colors often enough.

Lav_ish Eyeshadow Palette by ColourPop

Review:

Absolutely gorgeous packaging pigment and dazzling shimmers. If you want purple pay off this is the palette to use. It doesn't apply patchy and it lasts so long using a good eye primer plus it's plethora of shades in such a small palette makes it easily blendable for any purple looks you want to create. Packaging is paper covered so careful of pigment stains. Would 100% repurchase this palette.

14$

Mint To Be Eyeshadow Palette by ColourPop

Review:

Love a good minty green palette with some extraordinary shimmers and plus ultra mattes? Look no further than this fresh breath of minty goodness. The pigment is beautiful and the packaging is a hard durable plastic with a mirror so pigment stains are a thing of the past. This palette is Mint To Be in your collection if you're looking for that icey way to let your dentist know you flossed! Defininetly worth a repurchase.

14$

Superbloom Palette by Colourpop - discontinued

Review:

One of my favorite palettes ever the colors are so beautiful. Pigment is amazing and buildable the darker colors aren't patchy at all and it has the prettiest greens I've ever seen. Plastic packaging so nice and sturdy and because of the see through lid you don't even have to open it to decide if it's the colors you want. I love it and wish I would've bought ten more when they were still being sold if you snatched one up consider yourself lucky!

Sage The Day Eyeshadow Palette by ColourPop

Review:

A few pretty green and a lovely peach metallic however the rest of the palette with it's makes it feel like a potted plant and the shift shadow is very out of place. The metallics are still a struggle to get that real pow you want so if you're eyeing up the peach and greens I suggest purchasing their Jelly Much gel shadow in Sage Bundle. Packaging is paper so pigment stains are possible. Wouldn't repurchase since there are better options if I want these colors.

14$

Wanna Bee Eyeshadow Palette by ColourPop

Review:

Vibrant and a buttery formula makes this palette the talk of the hive. The nice warm browns and the yellows make for some stunning looks. Easy to apply and not patchy and all around a great option if you're in the market for some summer sun vibes. Paper wrap packaging so careful of pigment stains. Definitely would repurchase this.

14$

Uh-Huh Honey Eyeshadow Palette by ColourPop

Review:

The cousin of the one before filled with only summer sun vibes. The yellows have great pigment and are so smooth to blend a great eyeshadow palette. Not many shades in it other than yellow but it's not really a con. Great hardshell packaging so no worries about pigment stains. Looks great would repurchase 100 out of 100.

14$

Christmas Bake Shoppe Limited-Edition Holiday Set by Too Faced - Discontinued

Review:

Beautiful colors in every palette and contains the perfect nude and brown hues for a brown smokey eye or a fun neutral look with a pop of color. I'm not sure if the larger pans are a blush which is one of the only cons of this because it's not made clear on the packaging. The packaging is a good weight however it is paper wrapped so be careful with stronger pigments. At the time this pallette collection was released it was amazing deal because you got three eyeshadow palettes for half of what they would've costed seperatly. Too Faced also scents most of their products so they also smell really pleasant like a sweet cookie. If they released this collection again it is 100% worth buying.

Retro Eyeshadow Palette by NATASHA DENONA

Review:

A palette filled with pinks and burgandys very pretty mattes but the darker ones require a bit of building. The pigment itself is very smooth and works very nicely it doesn't contain much of a strong scent and the palette has a mirror. Its packaging is a hard sturdy plastic and the lid isn't floppy so when you open it it'll stay in the place you opened it to. I would not repurchase due to its single color story however it was very worth trying it out once and I love using it.

69$

Nouveau Eyeshadow Palette by ABH

Review:

The colors in this palette were very pretty despite being scattered I adore the color wisteria and the pigment in all of the shades are lovely and the eyeshadows don't have strong chemical smells. The packaging is excellent and contains a mirror though it is covered in a rough fabric like material it still has some paper parts. Would repurchase best palette ABH has by far.

55$
